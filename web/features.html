Schedule A. The full schedule of provisions

provision № 1

The .v100 container

Self-describing, tamper-evident vaults — authenticated header, per-chunk tags, truncation-proof stream, original filenames restored on opening. v1 vaults still honoured.

provision № 2

111 key-turning settings

Argon2id memory-hard derivation — a graded ladder of 108 fine notches (16 MiB × 1 turn → 1 GiB × 6 turns) plus standard, paranoid and auto-calibrated “max”. Stamped into every header; opens on any build.

provision № 3

The double-lock cascade

Optional AES-256-GCM ⟶ XChaCha20-Poly1305 — two independent ciphers in series. A break of either one still leaves the other holding the seal.

provision № 4

The keyfile press

A 256-bit physical second key (.v100key). The vault then demands both — combination in your head, key in your pocket. Guessing alone is dead.

provision № 5

Instant recombinate

Change a vault’s passphrase or keyfile in a blink — only the sealed head is re-pressed, the body is never re-encrypted. 10 GB moves as fast as 10 KB.

provision № 6

Shrink-wrap & folder filings

Optional gzip inside the vault, and whole folders filed as one tidy tar — sealed as a single document.

provision № 7

The examining tray

On opening, the head reads its facts aloud; texts, images and PDFs may be examined right on the counter — ≤ 25 MiB, never saved, swept away after.

provision № 8

The timekeeper

Device speed trials — XChaCha and AES-GCM throughput, Argon2id notch costs, and what the standard profile truly costs on your desk. Choose notches informed, not guessed.

provision № 9

Office security ops

Idle sweep policy, the Esc, Esc panic drill, and a clipboard furnace that burns minted combinations after 60 seconds.

provision № 10

Five inks × two shifts

Carbon, oxford, sepia, ledger and crimson inks over a day desk or night desk — remembered politely in your own storage, nowhere else.

provision № 11

One cloth, three bodies

Web counter, CLI engine and desktop box produce byte-interchangeable vaults — proven in the test lab both directions, every release.

provision № 12

Your own bureau

Self-host with a zero-dependency Node server or the Dockerfile. MIT — the paperwork is yours to print anywhere.
