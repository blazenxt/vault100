Annex D. Small machines of the bureau

instrument (a)

The keyfile press

Stamps a 256-bit physical second key. Keep it as a house key — somewhere apart, with a copy laid safely away.

instrument (b)

The examining glass

Reads only a vault's public header — form, cipher, turns of the key. No passphrase requested, no contents disturbed.

instrument (c)

The passphrase press

Cryptographically secure combinations, minted a moment ago, in this room and nowhere else.

combination quality — —
instrument (d)

The certificate of trust

Upon arrival this form opens a reference vault sealed by the desk application — proving the delivered machinery is genuine and compatible. The stamp, upper right, records the verdict.

instrument (e)

The office policy

After a spell of stillness the clerk sweeps the counter — every combination, filing and keyfile off the desk, the record book burned and re-opened. Esc, Esc holds a panic drill at any hour.

kept in this booth only — copies made from the annex are burned from the clipboard after 60 seconds.
instrument (f)

The timekeeper

Clocks this very device: the speed of both ciphers and the cost of a turn of the Argon2id key — so the notch you choose is an informed one, not a guess. Run in a minute; nothing leaves the room.

the stopwatch has not yet run on this desk.
instrument (g)

The quorum press

Breaks one secret into N stamped slips; any M of them reprint the secret, fewer than M learn nothing at all (Shamir over GF(2⁸), pressed in this room). Lodge the slips apart — a drawer, a ledger, two trusted hands — so no single theft, fire or faulty memory takes the combination with it. Slips are reprinted by the desk application as well: vault100 share split/join.

strike the slips
reprint the secret
 
the press stores nothing: slips exist only on this counter until swept (Esc, Esc burns them). Anyone holding M untampered slips learns the whole secret — guard each slip like a key, and retire the set when one goes missing.
instrument (h)

The courier's corner

Folds a short scrap — a quorum slip, an armor block, a note for a phone — into a stamped QR square the camera reads. Nothing travels a wire: the ink moves by eye and lens alone. Royal capacity ≈ 1,200 characters; beyond that, send a sealed file instead.

stamp the courier square
 
instrument (i)

The notary

Mints a personal seal (secret — guard it like a keyfile) and its stamp (public — share it freely). The seal endorses any vault with an ed25519 signature; anyone holding the stamp can attest the endorsement, here or at the desk application — vault100 notary mint/endorse/attest, byte for byte the same stamp. A doctored vault, a swapped file, a foreign seal — all refused, loudly.

mint the seal

one press strikes both pieces; the seal keeps 0600 hours — secrecy is its habit.

 
endorse a vault
 
attest the endorsement
attestation proves who sealed the paper and that not one byte moved since — it never reads what the paper says; for that, the combination still rules.
