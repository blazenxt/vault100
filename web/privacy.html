Writ № 100-P. The privacy writ — what the bureau keeps

§1

No accounts. The bureau does not know your name, your mail, nor your custom. There is nothing to sign, nothing to cancel.

§2

No uploads. Documents, combinations, keyfiles and vault bodies are processed wholly on your device — inside a Web Worker at the counter, or offline in the engine. The wire carries only this printed paperwork.

§3

No telemetry. No analytics, no cookies, no trackers, no beacons, no fingerprinting — and no advertising, ever. (Where a delivery network attempts to slip its own beacon into this sheet, our content policy stamps it out on arrival.)

§4

What is kept — three desk notes, in your own browser only: your chosen ink, your chosen shift (day/dark desk), and your sweep-clock seconds. Nothing else, nowhere else; these never travel.

§5

The clerk's record lives in your tab alone. It is burned by the idle sweep, by the Esc, Esc panic drill, by closing the page, or by your own hand (“sweep now”).

§6

Honest exception. The hosting premises (Railway) and the delivery network (Cloudflare) keep ordinary infrastructure logs — connection times and addresses. The bureau receives none of it, enriches none of it, and cannot tie any of it to a document, because documents never arrive.

§7

Memory is your office, not ours. Forgotten combinations cannot be recovered by anyone — §6 of the terms applies. This is a feature written in stone, not a flaw.

§8

Deletion. Everything the counter ever noted is erased by sweeping, by closing the tab, or by removing the installed app (which also burns the offline cache).

§9

Enquiries. Privacy matters are heard at the issues window; sensitive ones at private advisories.
