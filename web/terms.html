Writ № 100-T. Terms of counter service

§1

The grant. Vault100 is free and open-source software under the MIT license — use, copy, modify, merge, publish, distribute, sublicense and sell, keeping the notice with it.

§2

As is, as stamped. The bureau and its instruments are provided without warranty of any kind, express or implied — including fitness for a particular purpose. The authors are not liable for claims or damages arising from its use.

§3

Your keys, your kingdom. Combinations and keyfiles are yours alone to keep. The bureau holds no copies and can make no recoveries; loss of them is loss of the vault — final, and by design.

§4

Lawful counter. Bring only what the law of your land permits you to seal. The bureau serves privacy, not impunity.

§5

Opening hours. Continued availability of the hosted counter is hoped for but not promised. Those who need guarantees may run their own bureau — it is the same machinery, and it is free.

§6

Changes. The paperwork evolves through stamped, tagged releases. Vaults made today remain openable — format compatibility is a bureau oath.

§7

No data, no liability for data. Since nothing about you is collected (see the privacy writ), there is nothing of yours here to lose, leak, or subpoena.

§8

Conduct of the counter. Disputes are settled in the open: state them plainly at the issues window. The community covenant governs the manners.
